# Final-Project-GIS-Project-and-Programming-
This repository was created for the final project  in GIS Projects and Programming

Project Overview
This project examines environmental risks and environmental justice conditions in the City of Detroit, Michigan using a spatial, census‑tract‑level approach. The analysis focuses on understanding how environmental exposures and ecological burdens are distributed across neighborhoods and how these patterns relate to population distribution. Rather than relying on statewide or regional summaries, the project emphasizes fine‑scale spatial analysis to identify localized areas of elevated environmental risk that may be masked at broader scales.
Multiple data sources are integrated to capture different dimensions of environmental burden. These include census‑tract population data, ecological and regulatory environmental indicators (such as solid waste facilities, contaminated sites, and cleanup sites), and satellite‑derived air pollution measures (NO₂ and O₃). These variables are combined into composite indices representing cumulative environmental burden, allowing for a more holistic assessment of environmental risk than single‑indicator analyses.
The project applies exploratory spatial analysis and percentile‑based classification methods (25th, 50th, and 75th percentiles) to identify census tracts with moderate, high, and very high environmental burden. Population data are then used to estimate how many residents live in high‑burden areas, providing a population‑weighted environmental justice perspective. Overall, this project demonstrates how spatial analysis and integrated environmental datasets can be used to evaluate environmental justice concerns at the neighborhood scale and support data‑driven decision‑making.
